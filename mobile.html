MEET
ROBERT

IT professional; in-demand skills; gamer.

He wants to move his 401(k) to each new employer.

He’ll roll over his 401(k) to a new employer’s plan.

ADVANTAGES

  • Assets retain tax-advantaged growth potential.
  • Easier tracking and management of combined assets.
  • Loans may be allowed.
  • Assets typically protected from creditor claims.

KEEP IN MIND

  • Possible waiting period to move money to new plan.
  • New employer will control:
    • Investment options.
    • Access to retirement savings.

She doesn’t have time to worry about her 401(k) right now.

She’s leaving her 401(k) in her former employer’s plan.

ADVANTAGES

  • No immediate action required.
  • Assets retain tax-advantaged growth potential.
  • Access to investments not available outside of plan.
  • Assets typically protected from creditor claims.

KEEP IN MIND

  • Additional contributions typically not allowed.
  • Must maintain relationship with former employer.
  • Plan may not allow you to keep it there.
  • Former employer will control:
    • Investment options.
    • Access to retirement savings.
